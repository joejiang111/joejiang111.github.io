Families Are Still Struggling to Pay Medical Costs Despite Nearly Universal Health Coverage in Massachusetts

Massachusetts is a national leader in its pursuit of universal health care coverage. More than 97% of Massachusetts residents have health insurance. In spite of this, affordability issues persist across all groups regardless of age, gender, race, health status, or family income.

Below, CHIA looks at four different areas that are affecting Massachusetts residents:
Medical Bills, Unexpected Medical Bills, Medical Debt, and High Deductible Health Plans.





Medical Bills
Of all Massachusetts residents with health insurance, 15% experienced problems paying medical bills in 2019.

The problems are associated with various types of health care services.

Most often, two or more types of care were reported.

And insured residents often faced serious financial consequences.



Families with moderate incomes were especially burdened.

FPL stands for Federal Poverty Level.
Unexpected Medical Bills
Increasingly, patients will also receive a bill that they weren't anticipating.

Among Massachusetts' insured residents, 37% received an unexpected medical bill in the last 12 months.
45% have received 3 or more unexpected medical bills.
Families with children were more likely to have unexpected medical bills than those without children.
Among insured residents, those who received unexpected medical bills are 3x more likely to experience problems paying than those who do not receive those bills.

High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)
HDHPs represent a growing share of the private health insurance market in Massachusetts.

Residents with family income over 300% of FPL were more likely than those with lower family income to enroll in an HDHP, regardless of chronic condition.
HDHP members reported high rates of affordability issues than other privately insured residents.
HDHP enrollment was associated with a higher rate of affordability issues when examined by family income and chronic condition status
HDHP members with both family income below 300% of the FPL and one or more chronic conditions, 84% reported having affordability issues
Even among HDHP members with higher income and no chronic conditions, 41% experienced an affordability issue
